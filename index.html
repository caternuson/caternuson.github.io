ABOUT

Aerospace Enginerd living and climbing in the Pacific Northwest.



AIRPLANES & AERODYNAMICS

The aerodynamics of flight has always fascinated me. In pursuit of this interest I obtained a BS and MS in Aeropsace Engineering from Texas A&M University in 1995/97. I subsequently worked at The Boeing Company for 15 years carrying out wind tunnel testing. My primary responsibility was developing and supporting flow visualization techniques. Really fun stuff and really beautiful data - none of which I can show. Proprietary secret stuff and what not. Sorry.

I also know how to fly airplanes.

I have a Light Sport Pilot license. Here is a video of me practicing landings at Arlington (KAWO) where I took my lessons. The airplane was a Tecnam Sierra P2002, very fun to fly. Unfortunately the FBO I flew out of closed shop and I haven't been able to find a new place to fly. Hopefully I'll get back up in the air again some day.

MOUNTAINS

I climb mountains. Lots of mountains. Photos of some of my various climbing adventures can be found on my Flickr page. Here is a video of me climbing Mt. Redoubt in the North Cascades. Other places where I have adventured to include:

  • Nepal
  • Greenland
  • Australia
  • Colorado
  • Sierra Nevadas
and that's just scratching the surface.

I have always been drawn to the outdoors, but mountains have a special draw for me. In 2006 I got more serious about things and took a series of classes offered by BOEALPS, starting with the Basic Climbing Class. The following year I took the Intermediate Climbing Class which really opened up the alpine to me. Since then I have been helping instruct in these classes and voraciously enjoying the amazingly beautiful mountains of the Cascade range - a lifetime supply of blissful hinterlands, glaciated and rocky.
